Craftsmanship or mass production? Preserving the traditional value of the historical Van Phuc village.

Nguyễn Đại Nghĩa - 28 Aug 2021

A Field trip report for the HASS1050 course.

End of April, under the blazing sun, we ventured into an ancient village of Ha Noi, a one-of-a-kind place famous for its silk-making tradition. It was a fascinating field trip to Van Phuc village - a more than thousand years old destination, in Ha Dong, Ha Noi. We have had the chance to witness the spectacle of the traditional craftsmanship in weaving silk and harmonizing it into ingenious, delicate, and lustrous products of human genius.

A legend origin.

The village was originally named Van Bao, yet due to a naming conflict with the emperor, which is inhibited at that time, it has to rename itself to Van Phuc. The silk-making tradition of the village could be traced back to more than a millennium ago (about more than 1100 years ago) by Lã Thị Nga - wife of the Cao Biền. In the early day, she wandered across the land and help people making a living till her arrival at Van Bao, where she decided to stay and brought the techniques of mulberry farming and silkworm harvesting to teach the inhabitants there. After her death, she was praised as the tutelary spirit (thành hoàng) of the village and was worshipped in the Van Phuc shrine. Since then, the legend of Ha Dong silk was started and lasted for more than a millennium. With its top-notch quality product, Van Phuc and its silk have immersed themselves into literature, poems, and hymns.

Craftmanship or mass production?

The unique essence of Van Phuc silk has long been proven. With its aesthetic and delicate, Ha Dong silk was among the selected materials to make royalty attire. Following the French colonization, this silk has appeared in the international colonial exhibition in 1931 (Marseille) and 1932 (Paris) and was recognized as one of the most ingenious goods in Indochina.

The silk weaved in Van Phuc is mostly handcrafted by skilled artisans, whereas the only automated component is the electric motor to generate motion for the whole system. The crafting was meticulous and required years of experience to sense the diminutive width of single silk yarn to make any adjustment needed. Each yarn is formed by many threads and as told by a senior artisan there: a yarn is worth 4 to 5 threads, and a thread requires 11 cocoons - such dedicated and mesmerizing effort!

"Một nong tằm bằng năm nong kén, một nong kén bằng chín nén tơ."

More than the meticulousness is the aspire in creating beautiful patterns for the silk, which elevates the novel nature of the fabric. These patterns are embedded in the weaving process through a complex series of hole-punched cards that derives the motion of the machine to create the designs.

Despite all the enthusiasm in each product, Van Phuc silk is facing many challenges in keeping up with the rapid pace of development in modern society. With the rise of mass production, cheaper artificial silk products made of polyester are flourishing in the market, especially those from China with diverse patterns and designs. The quality is also more consistent due to the advantage of repetitive manufacturing, artificial silk products rapidly reach the local market and satisfy the majority of needs of consumers. These factors impose a significant risk on traditional silk as a direct competitor, where the advantages of artificial silk flat out the many uniqueness of Van Phuc silk.

Preserve the traditional values.

Preserving a historical craft village isn't a mere responsibility. For the moment, Van Phuc silk products are able to attract a stable customer base. Foreign tourists fascinating with unique local goods or the Vietnamese preferring elegant high-quality domestic products sustainably consume these silk crafts.

However, for long-term sustainable development, the signature of Van Phuc should be more than just a name, but a destination, an identity of craftsmanship. The value may not lie in the product itself but the aspire to create it - the "Ha Dong silk", in the craftsmanship of skilled artisans weaving it. These identities give the products a unique advantage comparing to machinery products with indistinguishable quality and no value embedded inside.

Van Phuc has a thousand-year history of making high-quality silk, and these values have been passed to many generations. Many goods from the village now featured the branding "Van Phuc silk" or "Ha Dong silk" - an initial step to affirm and distinguish the quality of the products here.
